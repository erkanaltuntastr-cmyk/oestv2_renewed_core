Mark Scheme (Open-Ended)

Q73

  • Mentions dharma as living in the right way.
  • Mentions karma as actions having consequences.
  • Mentions samsara cycle of birth, death, rebirth.
  • Mentions moksha or nirvana as freedom from the cycle.
  • Includes practices like meditation or prayer.

Q74

  • Beliefs can lead to standing up for human rights.
  • Beliefs can lead to charity or helping the poor.
  • Daily life rules about what to wear or eat.
  • Daily life rules about worship/prayer or festivals.

Q75

  • Atheism: belief there is no God or gods.
  • Atheists use science, reason, human experience.
  • Humanism: non-religious worldview focusing on people, reason, compassion.
  • Humanists believe this life is the only one and reject God/heaven/hell.

Q76

  • Trace history back to prophet Abraham.
  • Judaism, Christianity, and Islam began in the Middle East.
  • Share belief in one God (monotheism).
  • Share holy books and moral rules.

Q77

  • Helps answer big questions like why we are here.
  • Helps with questions about suffering.
  • Gives a sense of purpose or guidance in life.
  • Links to ideas of afterlife or rebirth.

Q78

  • Life is sacred and should be treated with respect.
  • Includes respect for human and animal life.
  • Belief in an inner self, spirit or soul.

Q79

  • Christianity grew from Judaism.
  • It began in the 1st century CE.
  • Followers believe Jesus is God's son and a Saviour.

Q80

  • Humanist ceremonies are for births, weddings, funerals.
  • They replace religious ceremonies.
  • They fit with humanist values and focus on people.

Q81

  • Religious Education is the study of different religions.
  • Includes beliefs and practices.
  • Looks at how religions influence people’s lives.

Q82

  • Community means people of different cultures and faiths.
  • They live respectfully together in one community.

Q83

  • Revelation is when God reveals or shows His message.
  • Can be through scripture or prophets.

Q84

  • Pilgrimage is a journey for religious reasons.
  • It can include visiting a holy place.
